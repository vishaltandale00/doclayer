1. RLMs, framed claim-first

Reasoning Language Models aren't a new species; they're what happens when the harness around an LLM gets reflective enough to grade its own steps.

The eval is the loop, not the token. Once you accept that, every architectural choice downstream — memory, tool-use, multi-pass refine — collapses into one question: what are you scoring at each step?

A claim-first opener earns the reader's patience. So: the meta-harness is an architect that rewrites itself

reviewing · §3.2 · 240 words

3.2 The chassis is the contract

A meta-harness isn't a tool that improves a document — it's the chassis that lets two writers and a model collaborate without one of them having to translate for the other. The architect emits the workspace; the workspace emits the events; the events route to whichever harness has the right surface to receive them.

When the chassis is right, the work collapses into one motion: claim, evidence, counter — all in the same edit.

This is what people miss about self-improving systems: the improvement isn't in the model, it's in the shape of the loop. A model that hill-climbs against a fixed target is just a better executor. A model whose harness rewrites the target is doing something else entirely — and the chassis is what tells you which.

Every recursive structure has the same failure mode: the floor it converges to is the one defined by the very thing doing the converging. A meta-harness escapes this by letting the user be part of the convergence condition.

guide · scenario 09

The review loop, end-to-end. One full protocol cycle, ~30 seconds, both sides visible.

0–4s · type with AI inline augment

Vishal types §3.2 live. As he writes, the AI augments two clauses inline — a citation badge attaches; a hedge gets a contradiction nudge. The augments are atoms in the manifest, not transient suggestions.

4–7s · packet pip

Vishal pauses. The architect packs the section as a review packet: text + augments + provenance + a target reviewer. A packet pip flies across the gutter toward Akhil’s shell.

7–10s · notification to akhil

Akhil’s shell receives the packet. The intent-state badge shifts: idle → incoming packet → shifting into review. The lifecycle ribbon advances the “review” chip.

10–17s · akhil’s harness reshapes

Akhil’s shell visibly reshapes: outline collapses to make room; review rail expands. The packet binds into Akhil’s editor with full provenance — Vishal’s augments are visible inline, dim-styled.

17–22s · comments compose

Akhil composes comments. Some are accept-with-edit, some are reject-with-reason. The architect packs comments + dispositions as a return packet.

22–26s · notification back

The return packet flies back across the gutter to Vishal. His harness shifts to “iterate-mode” — a diff rail appears alongside the editor with Akhil’s comments inline.

26–30s · vishal iterates with comments

Vishal applies revisions. Some comments resolve as accepts; others spawn back-and-forth threads. The loop closes; both harnesses return to their original intents. The chain is one packet richer.

what this provesThe protocol is symmetric. Either side’s harness reshapes on receive, not on send. The review loop is the actual mechanism the meta-harness arranges.
