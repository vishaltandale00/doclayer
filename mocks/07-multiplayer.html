0–5s · two shells

Two shells side-by-side. Vishal’s shell is research-heavy — evidence rail, citation density. Akhil’s shell is voice-heavy — density meter, voice sampler. Same doc, two harnesses.

5–10s · structurally different per prior

The harnesses don’t just have different content — they have different layouts. Different panels, different gutters, different intent-state badges. Layout is a function of prior.

10–14s · both writing different sections

Both authors type live. Vishal in §2; Akhil in §3.2. Cursors don’t collide — they aren’t even in the same paragraph. The harnesses share grammar, not viewport.

14–18s · sync gutter

A sync gutter between the two shells lights up as deltas merge through the chain runtime. Each delta is a typed atom event, not a text patch.

18–22s · cross-routing as reviews

Akhil drops a comment on §2. It appears in Vishal’s shell — not in Vishal’s editor, but as a packet in his review rail. Cross-collab is routed, not broadcast.
